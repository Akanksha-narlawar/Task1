About

Hanuman is one of the most respected and powerful deities in Hindu tradition. He is known for his extraordinary strength, intelligence, courage, and deep devotion to Rama. Hanuman plays an important role in the great epic Ramayana, where he becomes one of the greatest supporters of Rama in the fight against evil. Because of his loyalty, bravery, and humility, Hanuman is worshipped by millions of people around the world. Hanuman is often shown with the face of a monkey and a strong human body. He carries a mace (gada), which represents strength and protection. He is also known as Anjaneya because he is the son of Anjana and the wind god Vayu. According to tradition, Hanuman was blessed with many divine powers when he was born. These powers included the ability to fly, change his size, and move with great speed. As a child, Hanuman was very playful and curious. One famous story tells how he once saw the rising sun and thought it was a fruit. Because of this, he jumped high into the sky to catch and eat it. This shows how powerful he was even at a young age. However, his playful behavior sometimes caused trouble, so the sages placed a blessing that he would forget his powers until someone reminded him. This story teaches an important lesson that every person has hidden strength within them, but sometimes they need encouragement to discover it. Hanuman later became one of the most trusted followers of Rama. In the Ramayana, when Sita was taken away by the demon king Ravana, Rama began searching for her. During this time, Hanuman met Rama and immediately became devoted to him. Hanuman promised to help Rama find Sita and defeat Ravana. One of the most famous moments in the Ramayana is when Hanuman crossed the ocean to reach Lanka in search of Sita. This journey was extremely difficult, but Hanuman used his strength, courage, and intelligence to overcome every obstacle. He grew in size and leaped across the ocean with great power. When he reached Lanka, he carefully searched the city and finally found Sita in a garden called Ashoka Vatika. Hanuman respectfully introduced himself to Sita and gave her a ring from Rama as proof that he was sent by him. This brought great hope to Sita, who had been waiting for help. Hanuman then returned to Rama with the news that Sita was safe. His bravery and dedication played a very important role in the success of Rama’s mission. Another famous story about Hanuman happened during the great battle between Rama and Ravana. During the war, Rama’s brother Lakshmana was seriously injured. The only way to save him was to bring a special herb from the Sanjivani Mountain. Hanuman quickly flew to the mountain, but he could not identify the exact herb. Instead of wasting time, he lifted the entire mountain and carried it back to the battlefield. This act saved Lakshmana’s life and showed Hanuman’s quick thinking and dedication. Hanuman is admired not only for his strength but also for his humility. Even though he performed many great deeds, he never became proud. He always believed that his powers were meant to serve Rama and help others. This quality makes him a symbol of devotion and selfless service. Hanuman also represents courage and confidence. His life teaches people to face difficulties bravely and never give up. Many people pray to Hanuman for strength, protection, and success in their lives. Because of his noble qualities, Hanuman continues to inspire millions of people today. Temples dedicated to him can be found all across India and in many other parts of the world. Festivals such as Hanuman Jayanti celebrate his birth and remind people of his heroic deeds. The story of Hanuman teaches timeless lessons about devotion, courage, humility, and service. These values are important for building strong character and living a meaningful life. Hanuman’s example encourages people to remain loyal to their values, help others in need, and stay strong even in the face of challenges.

Lesson of Hanuman

The life of Hanuman offers many powerful lessons that guide people to live a meaningful and righteous life. Hanuman is respected for his extraordinary strength, courage, wisdom, and deep devotion to Rama, and his story is mainly described in the great epic Ramayana. Through his actions, Hanuman teaches important values such as devotion, loyalty, humility, courage, discipline, and selfless service. His devotion to Rama shows the importance of dedication and faith in fulfilling our duties without expecting rewards. Hanuman’s courage is seen when he bravely crossed the ocean to reach Lanka in search of Sita, who had been taken by Ravana. Even in dangerous situations, he remained calm and determined, teaching us that courage means facing difficulties with confidence. Hanuman also represents humility because, despite his immense powers, he never became proud and always respected others. Another important lesson from his life is selfless service. When Lakshmana was seriously injured in battle, Hanuman brought the healing herb from the Sanjivani Mountain, even carrying the entire mountain when he could not find the exact herb. This act shows his dedication and willingness to help others without hesitation. Hanuman’s life also teaches confidence and self-belief, reminding us that every person has hidden strength and potential within them. His discipline and focus helped him complete his mission successfully, showing that determination and hard work lead to success. Hanuman also demonstrated intelligence and wisdom when he carefully searched Lanka and comforted Sita with hope and reassurance.Even today, millions of people are inspired by his example, and festivals like Hanuman Jayanti celebrate his birth and remind people of his noble qualities. By following the values shown by Hanuman—devotion, courage, humility, discipline, wisdom, compassion, and service—people can develop strong character, overcome challenges, and live a life filled with purpose, positivity, and goodness.

Festival

The festival of Hanuman Jayanti is one of the most important and widely celebrated festivals in India. It marks the birth anniversary of Hanuman, the devoted follower of Rama and one of the most respected figures in Hindu tradition. Hanuman Jayanti is celebrated with great devotion, enthusiasm, and spiritual energy by millions of devotees across the country and in many parts of the world. The festival reminds people of Hanuman’s courage, strength, loyalty, humility, and unwavering devotion to Rama. These qualities make Hanuman an inspiring role model for people of all ages. Devotees celebrate this festival to honor his birth and to remember the valuable lessons that his life teaches about devotion, discipline, and selfless service. The story of Hanuman is mainly described in the sacred epic Ramayana, where he plays a crucial role in helping Rama defeat the demon king Ravana and rescue Sita. According to Hindu beliefs, Hanuman was born to Anjana and Kesari, and he is also believed to be the spiritual son of Vayu, the wind god. Because of this, Hanuman is sometimes called “Pavan Putra,” meaning the son of the wind. From a young age, Hanuman possessed extraordinary powers, intelligence, and courage. Many stories describe his childhood adventures and the divine blessings he received from various gods. Hanuman Jayanti is celebrated on different dates in different regions of India, depending on local traditions and calendars. In many parts of North India, it is observed on the full moon day of the Hindu month of Chaitra, which usually falls in March or April. In other regions, the festival may be celebrated at different times of the year. Regardless of the date, the spirit of the celebration remains the same—devotees gather to honor Hanuman and express their devotion through prayers, rituals, and community celebrations. On Hanuman Jayanti, temples dedicated to Hanuman are beautifully decorated with flowers, lights, and colorful banners. Devotees wake up early in the morning, take a bath, and visit temples to offer prayers. They bring offerings such as flowers, coconuts, fruits, and sweets. One of the most common offerings is a special sweet called Laddu, which is believed to be a favorite of Hanuman. Devotees also offer sindoor (vermilion), oil, and garlands to Hanuman idols as a symbol of respect and devotion. The atmosphere in temples is filled with the sound of bells, devotional songs, and chanting of sacred mantras. A very important part of Hanuman Jayanti celebrations is the recitation of sacred texts dedicated to Hanuman. Devotees often read or listen to the Hanuman Chalisa, a famous devotional hymn written by Tulsidas. This hymn praises Hanuman’s strength, wisdom, and devotion to Rama. It is believed that chanting the Hanuman Chalisa brings courage, protection, and positive energy into one’s life. In addition to this, many devotees also read sections from the Ramayana, particularly the Sundara Kanda, which describes Hanuman’s heroic journey to Lanka in search of Sita. Fasting is another common practice during Hanuman Jayanti. Many devotees observe a fast throughout the day as a sign of devotion and self-discipline. Some people eat only fruits and milk, while others refrain from eating until evening prayers are completed. Fasting is believed to purify the mind and body and strengthen one’s spiritual connection with Hanuman. After the prayers and rituals are completed, devotees break their fast and share prasad, which is the blessed food offered to the deity. Processions and cultural programs are also organized in many places during Hanuman Jayanti. Large groups of devotees gather to sing devotional songs known as bhajans and kirtans. These songs praise Hanuman’s bravery and devotion to Rama. In some areas, dramatic performances and storytelling events are organized to depict scenes from the Ramayana, especially the heroic deeds of Hanuman. These performances help younger generations learn about the cultural and spiritual significance of Hanuman’s life. One of the most famous stories associated with Hanuman tells how he helped Rama during the battle against Ravana. When Rama’s brother Lakshmana was severely injured, Hanuman was asked to bring a powerful medicinal herb from the Himalayan mountains. Unable to identify the exact herb, Hanuman lifted the entire mountain and carried it back to the battlefield. This incredible act demonstrated his strength, dedication, and determination to save Lakshmana’s life. Stories like this are often retold during Hanuman Jayanti celebrations to remind people of Hanuman’s devotion and bravery. Hanuman Jayanti also carries deep spiritual meaning. Hanuman is considered a symbol of devotion, loyalty, humility, and service. Devotees believe that praying to Hanuman can remove fear, obstacles, and negative energies from life. Many people worship Hanuman to gain strength, courage, and confidence. Students pray to him for wisdom and focus, while others seek his blessings for protection and success in their endeavors.
